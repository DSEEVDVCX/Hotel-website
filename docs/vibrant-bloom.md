# Vibrant Bloom

## An Algorithmic Philosophy

Vibrant Bloom is a generative aesthetic where Islamic geometric tiles are not placed — they *bloom*. The algorithm plants seeds across the canvas; each seed germinates into a flower whose petals follow strict rotational symmetry (6-fold and 8-fold), expanding outward like a living organism breathing. The bloom is not instant: it swells from a single point, reaches full radiance, then contracts and dissolves — only for new seeds to sprout elsewhere. This is the product of deep computational expertise: a meticulously crafted algorithm where every growth rate, petal count, and decay curve was refined through countless iterations.

The foundational principle is **seeded germination**. Each bloom begins as a microscopic point — a seed carrying a symmetry order (6 or 8), a hue, and a growth destiny. As the bloom ages, its petals unfurl following trigonometric curves: each petal is a lobe defined by `cos(angle * symmetry)`, creating the characteristic star-flower hybrids found in Alhambra tilework. The petals do not fill solidly; they are rendered as concentric rings of decreasing radius, producing a luminous, layered texture like light passing through stained glass. Every parameter — the lobe depth, ring spacing, petal taper — was painstakingly calibrated by a master of computational aesthetics.

**Color emerges from bloom age, not assignment.** Young blooms burn with saturated gold and emerald — vivid, alive, reaching. As they mature, their colors deepen and cool toward bronze and deep jade. In their final moments, they fade to ghostly silver-white, dissolving into the dark substrate. This age-to-color mapping was the product of painstaking calibration, where every gradient stop was carefully chosen to produce resonant, living beauty. The palette is Andalusian: gold, emerald, terracotta, indigo — each bloom claiming a hue from a weighted random pool.

The system maintains **temporal balance** — never too many blooms at once, never empty. A population controller limits concurrent blooms to 8-12, ensuring each has space to breathe. When one fades, another seed germinates within moments, maintaining visual continuity. The bloom lifecycle curve (ease-out growth, ease-in decay) was meticulously tuned so the garden feels organic, never mechanical. This is the mark of a master-level generative algorithm: life and death in mathematical rhythm, beauty that breathes.
